What Are the Most Common LG Dishwasher Problems?

LG dishwashers are popular in Toronto condos and homes alike, and we see consistent patterns across the LDB and LDT series models. The top problems our technicians encounter include:

  • OE error — drain fault — The dishwasher cannot drain within the set time. Filter blockage is the first thing to check.
  • IE error — inlet water error — The machine is not filling. Often a closed water supply valve or a faulty water inlet valve solenoid.
  • HE error — heating error — Water is not reaching wash temperature. The heater assembly or thermistor has failed.
  • FE error — overfill error — The water level sensor or inlet valve is stuck open, overfilling the tub.
  • Loud grinding noise during drain — A foreign object is caught in the drain pump impeller.
  • Poor cleaning performance — Typically spray arm blockage, low water pressure, or a worn wash motor.
  • Door not closing or latch failure — LG's plastic door latch can crack over time, especially in units with heavy door use.

What Does the LG Dishwasher OE Error Mean and How Is It Fixed?

OE is LG's drain error code and it is the single most common reason Toronto residents call us for LG dishwasher service. The code appears when the dishwasher's control system determines that the water level has not dropped sufficiently during the drain cycle.

Our approach to an LG OE error follows a clear diagnostic path. First, we clean the mesh filter and cylindrical coarse filter at the bottom of the tub — blocked filters are responsible for roughly 60% of OE errors we encounter. Next, we check the drain hose routing for kinks and verify the drain connection at the sink or garbage disposal. If the filter and hose are clear, we test the drain pump motor directly with a multimeter and check for foreign objects in the impeller.

How Do We Diagnose and Repair an LG Dishwasher?

1

Record the error code

We note the active error code and enter the LG service diagnostic mode to check for any stored fault history on the control board.

2

Clean and inspect the filter assembly

The mesh filter and coarse filter are removed, cleaned thoroughly, and inspected for damage. A cracked filter basket can allow debris to reach the pump.

3

Test the drain pump and wash motor

We apply voltage directly to the drain pump motor to confirm it runs freely. We also check the wash motor's amperage draw, which can reveal a worn motor before it fails completely.

4

Check inlet valve and water pressure

The water inlet valve solenoid is tested for correct resistance. We also check the incoming water pressure — LG dishwashers require at least 20 PSI for proper operation.

Warning: Never reach into the filter sump area of a powered dishwasher to clear a blockage. The drain pump impeller spins at high speed and will cause serious injury. Always disconnect power at the wall socket or breaker before accessing any internal component.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does the OE error code mean on an LG dishwasher?
OE means the LG dishwasher has detected a drain error — water is not draining within the expected time. Clean the mesh filter at the bottom of the tub first. If the error persists, the drain pump motor or the drain hose likely requires professional attention.
How much does LG dishwasher repair cost in Toronto?
LG dishwasher repairs in Toronto typically cost $165–$320 CAD including parts and labour. Drain pump replacement ranges from $195–$260, while a direct-drive motor or control board repair can approach $300–$350.
Why is my LG dishwasher making a loud noise during the drain cycle?
A loud noise during draining usually means a foreign object — a small utensil, a piece of glass, or a food stone — has gotten into the drain pump impeller. The pump must be accessed and cleared to prevent motor burnout.
