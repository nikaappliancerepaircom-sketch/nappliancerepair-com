Why Won't My Whirlpool Washer Drain?

Whirlpool produces both top-load and front-load washing machines, and the drain failure diagnosis differs between the two. Understanding which type you have helps narrow the cause immediately.

Whirlpool top-load washers — The classic top-loader uses a lid switch as a safety interlock. If the switch fails, the machine fills and agitates but will not spin or drain because the control board thinks the lid is open. This is the leading cause of drain failures we see on Whirlpool top-loaders in Scarborough and North York households, particularly in the WTW model series.

Whirlpool front-load washers — Front-loaders (the Duet and WFW series) have a drain pump filter accessible from a panel at the front bottom of the machine. Coins, hairpins, and small items collect there and eventually block water flow completely. The door latch also plays a role — if the latch doesn't confirm a locked door, the spin and drain sequence won't engage.

What Are the Top Causes of a Whirlpool Washer Not Draining?

  • Lid switch failure (top-loaders) — The actuator tab breaks or the switch contacts wear out. The machine runs but will not transition to spin/drain. A tell-tale sign is no spin noise at all after the wash cycle ends.
  • Clogged coin trap / pump filter (front-loaders) — Debris accumulates in the filter housing between the tub and the pump, restricting flow. Regular cleaning every 3–6 months prevents this.
  • Failed drain pump motor — The motor wears out or seizes. You may hear a humming sound during the drain phase with no water movement.
  • Blocked drain hose — A kinked, clogged, or improperly installed drain hose prevents water from reaching the standpipe.
  • Drive belt failure (some top-loaders) — On belt-drive models, a broken belt prevents the pump from being driven. The motor runs but neither the drum nor the pump moves.
  • Control board or timer failure — The drain pump relay on the control board fails to activate, or on older machines the mechanical timer doesn't advance to the drain step.

How to Troubleshoot a Whirlpool Washer Not Draining

1

Test the lid switch (top-loaders)

With the washer mid-cycle and full of water, press the lid switch plunger down with a pen or pencil. If the spin sound engages, the switch is broken and needs replacement. Do this carefully — keep hands away from moving parts.

2

Clean the pump filter (front-loaders)

Open the access door at the front bottom of the machine. Place towels down. Slowly unscrew the filter cap to let water drain, then remove any trapped debris. Reinstall and run the Drain & Spin cycle.

3

Check the drain hose

Pull the washer slightly away from the wall. Look for sharp bends or kinks in the drain hose. Verify the hose is not inserted more than 6 inches into the standpipe (too deep can create a siphon that causes the machine to drain and refill continuously).

4

Listen for the pump during the drain cycle

Select a Drain & Spin cycle. Listen at the front of the machine for pump motor noise. A humming without water movement means the pump is seized or blocked. No sound at all points to a switch, board, or timer issue.

5

Call a technician if the above don't resolve it
