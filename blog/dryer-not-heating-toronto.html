Electric Dryer vs. Gas Dryer: Different Causes, Same Symptom

The symptom — dryer tumbles normally but clothes stay damp and cold — is the same for both electric and gas dryers, but the root cause differs by fuel type. Understanding which type you have is the first step to an accurate diagnosis.

Electric dryers use a resistance heating element (a coiled wire similar to a toaster element) powered by a 240V circuit. The heating circuit also contains a thermal fuse, a cycling thermostat, and a high-limit thermostat. Any one of these components failing open will kill all heat.

Gas dryers use a gas burner assembly to heat air. The key components are the igniter, which glows red-hot to ignite the gas, and the gas valve coils (solenoid coils), which open the valve to release gas when the igniter is hot enough. A failed igniter or failed coils both result in no heat despite the dryer operating normally otherwise.

What Are the Top Causes of a Dryer Not Heating in Toronto?

  • Blown thermal fuse (electric and gas) — The most common cause on any dryer. The thermal fuse is a single-use safety device that opens permanently when the dryer overheats. Almost always caused by a clogged vent.
  • Broken heating element (electric) — The resistance wire breaks, interrupting the heating circuit. The element may look intact visually, but a multimeter will show no continuity.
  • Failed gas valve coils (gas) — The coils energise the valve to release gas to the burner. When they fail, the igniter glows but no gas is released. The dryer fires briefly then goes cold.
  • Defective igniter (gas) — An old or cracked igniter fails to reach the temperature needed to trip the radiant sensor and open the gas valve.
  • Cycling thermostat failure (electric) — Regulates temperature during the cycle. If it fails open, the heating element never receives voltage.
  • Clogged dryer vent (root cause) — Not a part failure itself, but the root cause behind most thermal fuse failures. A blocked vent must always be cleared as part of the repair.

How Do Our Technicians Diagnose a No-Heat Dryer?

1

Confirm the drum spins normally

We verify the motor circuit is intact — if the drum spins freely, the problem is isolated to the heating circuit, not the motor, belt, or door switch.

2

Test the thermal fuse

The thermal fuse is tested first with a multimeter. A blown fuse reads no continuity and must be replaced. We also check exhaust temperature to confirm the vent is not restricted.

3

Test the heating element or igniter

On electric models, we check the element for continuity. On gas models, we observe the igniter — if it glows but the burner doesn't light, the valve coils are tested next.

4

Inspect and clear the vent

We inspect the full vent run from the dryer to the exterior exhaust cap. Lint-blocked vents are cleaned with a rotary brush kit before we complete the repair.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does my dryer run but not produce heat in Toronto?
A dryer that tumbles but produces no heat has almost certainly lost its heating circuit. On electric dryers the most common cause is a blown thermal fuse or a broken heating element. On gas dryers, the igniter may have failed or the gas valve coils may be defective, preventing gas from flowing to the burner.
How much does dryer not heating repair cost in Toronto in 2026?
Dryer repair for a no-heat issue in Toronto typically costs $150–$280 CAD including parts and labour. Thermal fuse replacement is at the low end ($150–$185), while heating element or gas valve coil replacement ranges from $200–$280.
Does a clogged vent cause a dryer to stop heating?
Yes. A blocked dryer vent causes the dryer to overheat, which blows the thermal fuse as a safety measure. If you replace the thermal fuse without clearing the vent, the new fuse will fail again within days. Always clear the vent as part of a no-heat repair.
