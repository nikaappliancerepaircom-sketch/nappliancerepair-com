What Is the Kenmore 665 Series and Why Is It So Common?

The Kenmore 665 is a line of built-in dishwashers that was manufactured primarily by Whirlpool under the Kenmore brand sold through Sears Canada. These machines dominated the Canadian appliance market throughout the 1990s and 2000s, and a remarkable number of them are still in service in Toronto homes — particularly in the older housing stock of Scarborough, Etobicoke, and the inner suburbs of North York.

Because the 665 series was made by Whirlpool, its parts cross-reference directly with Whirlpool, Maytag, Amana, and KitchenAid dishwasher components. This means parts are widely available and generally less expensive than European-brand equivalents, making repair cost-effective even on older units.

What Are the Most Common Kenmore Dishwasher Problems?

  • Door latch failure — not starting — The plastic latch assembly cracks or the latch switch fails. Without a confirmed closed-door signal, the machine refuses to start.
  • Control board failure — Power surges (common in older Toronto housing) can damage the electronic control board. The unit may be completely unresponsive or behave erratically.
  • Not cleaning — poor wash performance — Usually clogged spray arms, a worn wash motor, or a failing chopper blade assembly.
  • Not draining — standing water — Drain pump motor failure or a blocked drain path.
  • Water inlet valve failure — not filling — The solenoid valve wears out, preventing water entry. The machine will start but not fill.
  • Lower spray arm bearing worn — vibration and noise — A common wear item on older 665 models that causes significant rattling during operation.

How Do We Diagnose a Kenmore Dishwasher That Won't Start?

1

Test the door latch switch

We use a multimeter to test the door latch micro-switch for continuity. If it reads open when the door is closed, the latch assembly needs replacement.

2

Check the thermal fuse on the control board

Many Kenmore 665 models have a thermal fuse on the control board that blows in response to overheating. We test it for continuity as an early diagnostic step.

3

Test the water inlet valve

With the door latch confirmed functional, we test the inlet valve solenoid. No water entering the tub after startup points directly to a failed valve or a closed supply line.

4

Inspect the pump and motor assembly

We check the pump assembly for blockages and test the motor for correct amperage draw. An overloaded or seized motor will trip the thermal overload and prevent the cycle from completing.

Warning: Kenmore 665 dishwashers sold in Canada use a 120V connection, but always treat the appliance as live until you have verified the circuit breaker is off and the power cord is unplugged. The control board capacitors can retain a charge briefly after disconnection.
