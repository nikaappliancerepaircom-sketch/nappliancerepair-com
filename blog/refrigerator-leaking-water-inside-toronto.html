Why Is Water Pooling Inside My Refrigerator?

When homeowners in Toronto find a puddle at the bottom of the fridge compartment or water dripping from the ceiling of the freezer, the defrost drain is the first place to look. During every defrost cycle, frost melts off the evaporator coils and flows down a small drain tube into a pan underneath the appliance. If food debris, ice, or mould blocks that tube, the water has nowhere to go except back into the refrigerator itself.

A secondary cause is a failing water inlet valve — the solenoid-controlled valve that supplies water to your ice maker and water dispenser. When this valve cracks or the seal wears out, it can drip water into the bottom of the fridge even when you are not using those features.

What Are the Most Common Causes?

  • Clogged defrost drain: By far the most frequent culprit. Ice or food particles block the drain hole at the back of the freezer floor.
  • Damaged door gasket: A worn or torn door seal lets warm, humid air in. That moisture condenses and collects at the bottom.
  • Water inlet valve leak: Applies to fridges with an ice maker or water dispenser. A cracked valve body or degraded seal drips continuously.
  • Tilted refrigerator: Fridges should lean back slightly so the drain pan sits level. If the appliance is tilted forward, water spills forward instead of draining properly.
  • Cracked drain pan: The plastic tray under the fridge catches defrost water and lets it evaporate. A crack sends that water onto your floor or back toward the fridge interior.

How to Diagnose the Problem Before Calling

1

Look at where the water appears

Water at the very bottom of the fridge compartment almost always points to the defrost drain. Water behind the crisper drawers can indicate a door gasket issue. Water near the ice maker line points to the inlet valve.

2

Check the door gasket

Close the fridge door on a piece of paper. If you can pull it out easily, the gasket is not sealing properly and warm air is getting in.

3

Inspect the freezer floor

Remove the freezer baskets and look for a small drain hole at the back. If it is surrounded by ice, the drain is blocked.

4

Check the drain pan

Pull the fridge away from the wall and locate the drain pan at the bottom rear. Look for cracks or overflow.

5

Call a technician if you cannot identify the source

Hidden leaks around the sealed system or internal water lines require diagnostic tools. Our team at N Appliance Repair arrives with parts for the most common repairs.

Safety Note: If water has reached any electrical components — the control board, wiring harness, or outlet behind the fridge — unplug the appliance immediately and call a technician. Do not attempt to dry components while the fridge is still powered.

Can I Fix a Clogged Defrost Drain Myself?

A mild blockage can sometimes be cleared with warm water flushed through the drain hole using a turkey baster. However, if the drain tube itself is frozen solid inside the cabinet wall, or if the heater that warms the drain tube has failed, you will need a technician. Our lead tech Alex M. notes that about half of the Toronto calls his team gets for "water inside the fridge" turn out to involve a failed defrost drain heater — not just a simple clog — which requires opening the rear panel of the freezer to access.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is water pooling at the bottom of my fridge?
The most common cause is a clogged defrost drain. During the defrost cycle, water drains into a pan below the fridge. If the drain is blocked with ice or debris, water backs up into the interior instead.
How much does it cost to fix a leaking fridge in Toronto?
Most defrost drain clears or water inlet valve replacements run $140–$240 CAD in Toronto including parts and labour. A door gasket replacement is typically $120–$180 CAD.
Can a leaking fridge damage my kitchen floor?
Yes. Prolonged leaks can warp hardwood or laminate flooring and promote mould growth beneath the fridge. It is best to address leaks within a day or two of noticing them.
Is it safe to use a refrigerator that is leaking inside?
Water inside the fridge will not make food unsafe immediately, but standing water promotes bacteria and mould. If the leak is coming from below and reaching electrical components, unplug the fridge and call a technician.
