What to Have Ready Before the Technician Arrives

Preparing these things in advance speeds up the diagnostic process and helps ensure same-day completion:

  • Appliance model number: Usually on a sticker inside the door, behind the kick plate, or on the back. Note down the brand, full model number, and serial number if visible.
  • Error codes displayed: Take a photo of any error codes currently showing on the display before the appointment.
  • Description of when the problem started: Did it happen suddenly or gradually? After a specific event (power outage, new detergent, heavy load)? This context accelerates diagnosis.
  • Clear access to the appliance: Move any items from around the washer, dryer, or fridge to give the technician working room on all sides.
  • Prior service history: If the appliance has been serviced before, mention this — it can indicate whether we're dealing with a recurring issue or a new problem.

Which Repairs Are Typically Completed Same-Day

Our technicians complete 95% of repairs in a single visit. This is possible because our service vans are stocked with OEM parts for all major brands — Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, GE, Bosch, Frigidaire, Kenmore, Maytag, KitchenAid, and others.

Repairs typically completed same-day:

  • Fridge: thermostat, evaporator fan motor, defrost heater, door gasket, start relay, condenser cleaning
  • Washer: drain pump, lid switch, door latch, control board, drive belt, water inlet valve
  • Dryer: thermal fuse, heating element, cycling thermostat, gas valve coils, drum belt, idler pulley
  • Dishwasher: drain pump, door latch, spray arm, control panel, water inlet valve
  • Oven/Range: bake element, temperature sensor, igniter, convection fan motor

The rare exception: compressor replacements on refrigerators require ordering the specific compressor for your model and scheduling a follow-up appointment.
