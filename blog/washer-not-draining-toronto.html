Why Is My Washing Machine Not Draining?

A washer that stops mid-cycle with water standing in the drum has failed to engage its drain pump effectively. The drain pump is responsible for pushing water out of the tub through the drain hose and into your home's standpipe or laundry sink. When the pump is blocked, broken, or cannot activate, water stays in the drum and the cycle stalls.

The symptom looks the same whether you have a front-load or top-load washer, but the diagnosis pathway differs. Front-load washers have a user-accessible lint filter (usually behind a small access door at the front bottom of the machine) that collects coins, hairpins, and lint — and this filter is the cause of most front-loader drain failures. Top-load washers are more likely to have a lid switch failure that prevents the spin and drain sequence from activating.

What Are the Top Causes of a Washer Not Draining?

  • Clogged drain pump filter (front-loaders) — The most common cause on LG, Samsung, Bosch, and Whirlpool front-loaders. Coins, hairpins, and lint accumulate until water flow is completely blocked.
  • Blocked or kinked drain hose — A drain hose that is kinked behind the washer, pushed too far into the standpipe, or partially clogged with lint will prevent proper drainage.
  • Failed drain pump motor — The pump motor itself burns out or seizes. You may hear a humming sound during the drain attempt with no water movement.
  • Lid switch failure (top-loaders) — Top-load washers will not spin or drain unless the lid switch signals the control board that the lid is closed. A broken switch = no drain.
  • Door latch failure (front-loaders) — Similar to the lid switch — the machine will not proceed to the drain and spin cycle if it cannot confirm the door is locked.
  • Control board fault — Less common, but a board failure can cause the drain pump relay to never engage.

How to Check Your Washer Before Calling a Technician

1

Check the drain hose for kinks

Pull the washer away from the wall slightly. Look at the drain hose where it exits the machine. A sharp kink will prevent all drainage. Straighten the hose and try running the Spin/Drain cycle.

2

Clean the pump filter (front-loaders)

Locate the small access door at the bottom front of the machine. Place towels on the floor. Unscrew the filter cap slowly — water will flow out. Remove any debris from the filter cavity and reinstall. Try running the drain cycle again.

3

Check the standpipe or laundry sink

If the drain hose empties into a sink, check that the sink drain is clear. A blocked sink drain will cause water to back up into the washer drum.

4

Test the lid switch (top-loaders)

Press the lid switch plunger manually with a pen or your finger while the washer is running. If the drum engages when you press it, the switch is the problem and needs replacement.

Warning: Before cleaning the drain pump filter or accessing any internal components, disconnect the washer from power. Hot or soapy water under pressure will drain from the filter access port — use towels and a shallow pan to capture the overflow. Never attempt pump filter access while the washer is powered on.
