Why Is My Whirlpool Dishwasher Not Draining?

Standing water at the bottom of a Whirlpool dishwasher after a cycle is one of the most common service calls we receive. The causes, in order of how frequently our Toronto technicians encounter them:

  • Clogged filter: The cylindrical filter at the bottom of the tub traps food debris. When not cleaned regularly, it blocks water flow and prevents proper draining. Cleaning it takes two minutes and costs nothing.
  • Blocked drain hose: The drain hose that connects the dishwasher to the sink drain or garbage disposal can become kinked behind the cabinet or partially blocked with grease buildup.
  • Garbage disposal knockout plug: On new installations, if the dishwasher drain hose connects to a new garbage disposal, the factory knockout plug inside the disposal inlet must be removed. If forgotten, the dishwasher cannot drain at all.
  • Failed drain pump motor: When the filter and hose are clear but water still sits in the tub, the drain pump motor has usually failed. You may hear a humming noise as the motor tries to run.
  • Faulty drain solenoid: Older Whirlpool models use a drain solenoid rather than a dedicated pump. When the solenoid coil fails, the drain valve stays closed.

Why Is My Whirlpool Dishwasher Not Starting?

A Whirlpool dishwasher that does not start at all — no lights, no response — versus one that has power but will not begin a cycle are two different problems. David K., who handles Etobicoke and Mississauga Whirlpool calls, walks through the distinction: "No power at all usually means a tripped breaker or a blown thermal fuse. Power but no cycle usually means a door latch problem or a failed control board."

  • Door not fully latching: Whirlpool dishwashers will not start if the door switch does not detect a closed latch. Push the door firmly until you hear a click.
  • Door latch assembly failure: The plastic latch mechanism or the switch inside it can break, preventing the control board from receiving the door-closed signal.
  • Tripped thermal fuse: A safety fuse that blows if the dishwasher overheats. The fuse must be replaced — it does not reset.
  • Failed control board: The electronic control board manages all dishwasher functions. Control board failures on Whirlpool dishwashers 5–9 years old are a known issue.

How to Troubleshoot Your Whirlpool Dishwasher

1

Clean the filter

Remove the bottom rack. Twist the cylindrical filter counterclockwise and lift it out. Rinse under hot water and scrub with a soft brush. If the filter is heavily coated with grease, soak in warm soapy water for 10 minutes.

2

Check the drain hose

Pull the dishwasher out slightly and look at the drain hose connection at the back. Check for kinks. Also check where the hose connects to the sink drain or disposal — make sure it is not clogged.

3

Test the door latch

Close the door firmly and listen for a solid click. Try pressing Start — if the door is not fully latched, the dishwasher will not begin. If the latch feels loose or does not click, the latch assembly needs replacing.

4

Check the circuit breaker

Whirlpool dishwashers run on a 120V dedicated circuit. Check your breaker panel for a tripped breaker. Reset it and try again before calling a technician.

Warning: If your Whirlpool dishwasher is leaking from the door, check the door gasket immediately. A leaking door gasket that is not addressed can warp your kitchen cabinet floor and promote mould. Do not run more cycles until the source of the leak is identified.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why won't my Whirlpool dishwasher drain?
Whirlpool dishwasher drain failures are most commonly caused by a clogged drain filter, a blocked drain hose, or a failed drain pump motor. Check and clean the filter first. If standing water remains after cleaning the filter, the drain pump or hose needs professional attention.
Why won't my Whirlpool dishwasher start?
A Whirlpool dishwasher that will not start is usually a door latch problem (door not fully closing or latch switch failed), a tripped thermal fuse, or a failed control board. Check that the door closes and latches with a click first.
How much does Whirlpool dishwasher repair cost in Toronto?
Most Whirlpool dishwasher repairs in Toronto run $130–$280 CAD including parts and labour. Drain pump replacement is $170–$260 CAD. Door latch assembly is $120–$180 CAD. Control board replacement runs $200–$320 CAD depending on model.
How long do Whirlpool dishwashers last?
Whirlpool dishwashers typically last 9–12 years in normal household use. With regular filter cleaning and proper loading, many run longer. A Whirlpool dishwasher under 9 years old is almost always worth repairing unless the repair cost is very high.
