Why Brand Certification Matters

Every major appliance manufacturer trains and certifies technicians on their specific diagnostic systems, error codes, and repair procedures. A factory-certified Samsung technician, for example, has access to Samsung's internal service bulletins and knows which components fail most commonly on each model series — knowledge that general-purpose repair shops simply do not have.

At N Appliance Repair, our technicians maintain active certifications from Samsung, LG, Whirlpool, Bosch, GE, and Frigidaire. This means when your Samsung French door fridge throws a 22E or 40E error code, our technician already knows the most likely cause and the correct part number — reducing your repair time and the chance of a misdiagnosis.

We stock OEM (original equipment manufacturer) parts for all major brands in our service vans. Using genuine parts — not aftermarket substitutes — is the only way to guarantee the repair will last and is required for our 90-day warranty to apply. Typical repair costs range from $150 to $380 for most common faults, with the $80 diagnostic fee waived when you approve the work.
