The number one refrigerator complaint across Mississauga is ice maker failure, and hard water is the primary cause. Peel Region's Lake Ontario water supply carries calcium hardness that deposits inside the ice maker water line, the fill valve, and the ice maker module itself. The deposits restrict water flow until the ice maker stops producing, or they coat the ice maker mold causing cubes to freeze in irregular shapes that jam the ejector. We descale ice maker systems, replace fill valves when they're too fouled to clean, and test production rate after the repair. Homes with whole-home water softeners in Mississauga have far fewer ice maker problems — if yours is failing repeatedly, a softener may be worth the investment.

Compressor failures in Samsung and LG refrigerators are a growing repair category in Mississauga, particularly in units purchased between 2014 and 2019. Samsung's twin-cooling and Family Hub models had a compressor design that ran at high load ratios when the ambient kitchen temperature was elevated — a situation common in Mississauga's newer-construction homes where open-concept kitchens absorb heat from cooking surfaces and south-facing windows. LG's linear compressor technology has been reliable overall, but a specific batch of units from 2016-2018 had a welding defect that causes premature failure. We diagnose compressor faults accurately and advise on whether repair or replacement is the better economic choice.

Door seal deterioration is a repair we encounter frequently in Mississauga's older housing stock — the late-1980s and 1990s homes in Cooksville, Lakeview, and Port Credit where original appliances are still in service. An aging door gasket loses its magnetic attraction and allows warm, humid air to enter the refrigerator compartment. The result is ice buildup on the back wall of the fresh-food compartment, elevated compressor run time, and eventually a frost problem in the freezer that blocks airflow. We replace door seals on all major brands and test seal integrity with a dollar-bill test before closing the job.

Defrost system failures are a consistent repair call in Mississauga, particularly in top-freezer and bottom-freezer models that are five to ten years old. The defrost heater, defrost thermostat, and defrost timer work together to melt the frost that accumulates on the evaporator coils. When any component in this chain fails, frost builds up over weeks until the evaporator is completely blocked, airflow stops, and the refrigerator section warms up while the freezer remains cold. We diagnose which component has failed, replace it with an OEM equivalent, and manually defrost the evaporator before the job is complete.

Condenser coil cleaning is a maintenance item that most Mississauga homeowners have never done. The condenser coils sit underneath or behind the refrigerator and release heat from the refrigeration cycle into the room. Pet hair, dust, and carpet fibres accumulate on these coils over time — especially in homes with dogs and cats — insulating the coils and forcing the compressor to work harder and run longer than it should. We clean condenser coils as part of every refrigerator service call. A refrigerator with clean coils can run ten to fifteen percent more efficiently and the compressor runs cooler, which extends its lifespan substantially.

Local tip for Mississauga homeowners: If you live in one of Mississauga's 1990s detached homes and your refrigerator is positioned against an interior wall with the condenser coils on the bottom, pull the unit out and look at the coils at least once every eighteen months. In homes with pets, these coils can become completely coated with hair and dust in as little as one year, and a refrigerator running with fully blocked coils typically fails its compressor within two years. Cleaning takes five minutes and a vacuum cleaner.

Common Refrigerator Issues in Mississauga Homes

Mississauga's suburban landscape features spacious homes built between 2000 and 2020, many with full-size appliance suites that handle the demands of large multicultural families. Developments in Erin Mills, Meadowvale, and Churchill Meadows typically come equipped with matching Whirlpool, LG, or Samsung sets. Here are the refrigerator problems our technicians encounter most frequently in Mississauga service calls.

  • Not cooling properly: When a refrigerator runs but does not keep food cold, the cause is usually dirty condenser coils, a failed evaporator fan, a stuck defrost heater, or a sealed system issue like low refrigerant from a pinhole leak.
  • Freezer frosting up: Excessive frost in the freezer compartment indicates a defrost system failure — the defrost heater, defrost timer, or defrost thermostat has failed, preventing the automatic defrost cycle from melting ice off the evaporator coils.
  • Water leaking on the floor: A puddle under the fridge typically comes from a clogged defrost drain line, a cracked drain pan, or a leaking water inlet valve connected to the ice maker or water dispenser.
  • Making loud humming or clicking: A refrigerator that clicks repeatedly is trying and failing to start its compressor — often a bad start relay or compressor overload protector. Continuous humming may indicate a condenser fan obstruction.
  • Ice maker not producing ice: Ice maker failures stem from a frozen water fill tube, a faulty water inlet valve, a failed ice maker module, or water pressure below the 20 psi minimum required for proper fill. We check each component systematically.

Maintenance Tips to Prevent Future Refrigerator Repairs

Preventive maintenance costs nothing but a few minutes of your time, and it can save you hundreds of dollars in avoidable repairs. Here are our top recommendations for keeping your refrigerator running smoothly:

  • Clean condenser coils every 6 months: Dust-clogged coils make the compressor work harder and run hotter, reducing efficiency and shortening its life. Pull the fridge out, vacuum the coils underneath or at the back, and push it back.
  • Check door gaskets for air leaks: Close the door on a dollar bill — if it slides out easily, the gasket is not sealing properly. Worn gaskets force the compressor to run more, raising your electricity bill and causing temperature fluctuations.
  • Keep the fridge at the right temperature: Set the refrigerator to 3 to 4 degrees Celsius and the freezer to minus 18 degrees Celsius. Use a thermometer to verify — the built-in dial is often inaccurate, especially on older models.
  • Do not block internal air vents: Every refrigerator has air vents between the freezer and fridge compartments. Packing food too tightly blocks airflow and creates warm spots where food spoils faster.
  • Replace the water filter on schedule: If your fridge has a water dispenser or ice maker, change the filter every 6 months. A clogged filter reduces water flow, causes small or hollow ice cubes, and can introduce contaminants.

Regular maintenance extends the average refrigerator lifespan by 5 to 10 years. If you would like a professional annual maintenance checkup for any of your home appliances, call us at (437) 524-1053 — we offer maintenance visits across Mississauga.
