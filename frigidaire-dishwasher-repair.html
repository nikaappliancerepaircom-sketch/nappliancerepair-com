Common Frigidaire Dishwasher Issues in Toronto & GTA Homes

Frigidaire dishwashers are among the most common appliances in Toronto-area homes, and our technicians see the same failure patterns repeatedly. Understanding what goes wrong helps you know when to call for service before a small issue becomes a costly repair.

  • Standing water after cycle (i20 code): The i20 error means the dishwasher cannot drain within the required time. Usually caused by a blocked drain hose, a failed drain pump impeller, or a clogged filter. Toronto's older homes often have kinked drain hoses under the sink that worsen over time.
  • Dishes not getting clean: Spray arm nozzles clog with mineral deposits from hard water. A worn wash motor delivers less water pressure. The heating element may fail to reach the 120°F minimum needed to activate enzyme detergents. We test each subsystem to find the real cause.
  • Door will not latch or start: The door latch assembly on Frigidaire models experiences wear, particularly on units over 5 years old. A failed door switch sends no signal to the control board, so the cycle never initiates. Latch replacement is a fast, same-day fix.
  • Water leaking from door: The rubber door gasket dries and cracks over time, especially in kitchens with strong sunlight exposure. A warped tub or damaged door hinge can also cause leaks. In condos and stacked units, even a small leak can trigger water damage claims.
  • Control board not responding: Power surges, moisture ingress, or general component aging can cause the main PCB to fail. Symptoms include unresponsive buttons, a dead display, or cycles that start and stop randomly.

Maintenance Tips to Prevent Future Frigidaire Dishwasher Repairs

A few simple habits can extend your Frigidaire dishwasher's life significantly and prevent most common failures:

  • Clean the filter every 30 days: Frigidaire dishwashers have a self-clean or manual filter depending on the model. Manual filters must be removed and rinsed. A clogged filter is the single most common cause of the i20 error code and poor cleaning performance.
  • Run a dishwasher cleaner every 2 months: Affresh or similar citric acid-based cleaners dissolve mineral deposits, grease buildup, and detergent residue that accumulate inside the tub, spray arms, and pump.
  • Inspect the door gasket seasonally: Wipe the rubber gasket with a damp cloth and check for cracks or stiffening. A deteriorating gasket is an early warning of leaks. Replacement gaskets are inexpensive and easy to install.
  • Check spray arm rotation monthly: Spin both spray arms by hand — they should rotate freely with no wobble. Blocked nozzles reduce wash pressure dramatically. Clear blocked holes with a toothpick.
  • Use the right detergent: Frigidaire recommends Cascade or Finish powder or tabs for best results. Liquid detergent can leave residue and is not recommended for most Frigidaire models.
