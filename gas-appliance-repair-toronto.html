Gas Appliance Emergency Protocol

Gas appliances in Toronto homes — including Victorian semis in the Annex, post-war bungalows in East York, glass condo towers along the waterfront — combine combustion and pressurized fuel in ways that demand immediate action when something goes wrong.

  1. If you detect the rotten-egg odour of mercaptan (added to natural gas for leak detection) in your Toronto home, evacuate everyone immediately
  2. Do not operate any electrical switches, phones, or appliances — any spark can ignite accumulated gas
  3. Once outside, call 911 from a safe distance
  4. Contact Enbridge Gas Emergency at 1-866-763-5427 to report the leak
  5. Remain outside until Enbridge or fire services confirm it is safe to re-enter
