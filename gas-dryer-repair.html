Gas Dryer Ignition System — How It Works and How It Fails

Understanding the gas dryer ignition sequence explains why these appliances fail the way they do:

  1. The thermostat calls for heat: The dryer's cycling thermostat senses that the drum air temperature has dropped below the target and sends current to the gas ignition system.
  2. The igniter glows: Current flows through the silicon carbide (or silicon nitride) igniter, heating it to approximately 1,800 degrees Fahrenheit. This takes 30 to 60 seconds.
  3. The solenoid coils open the gas valve: Once the igniter reaches operating temperature, its electrical resistance drops, allowing enough current to flow through the gas valve solenoid coils. The coils magnetize and pull the valve open, releasing gas to the burner.
  4. The gas ignites: The hot igniter lights the gas stream, producing a visible flame that heats the air entering the drum.
  5. The flame sensor monitors: A radiant flame sensor verifies the presence of the flame. If the flame extinguishes unexpectedly, the sensor shuts the gas valve within seconds.

Each component in this sequence has its own failure mode: the igniter cracks and loses heat output, the solenoid coils lose magnetic strength and cannot fully open the valve, the flame sensor accumulates soot and cannot detect the flame, or the gas valve body develops an internal seal leak. Frequently Asked Questions

How does a gas dryer differ from an electric dryer?+

A gas dryer generates heat by burning natural gas through a burner assembly with an igniter, gas valve, solenoid coils, and flame sensor. Electric dryers use a resistive heating element instead. When a gas dryer runs without heating, the gas-specific ignition components are almost always responsible — not the drum, belt, or motor that both types share. N Appliance Repair specializes in these gas-specific systems.

What is the gas dryer ignition sequence?+

The thermostat signals for heat, current flows to the igniter which heats to approximately 1,800 degrees Fahrenheit, the igniter's dropping resistance allows current through the solenoid coils which magnetically open the gas valve, gas flows and ignites, then the flame sensor confirms stable combustion. Each component has a distinct failure mode that N Appliance Repair technicians diagnose in sequence.

Why does N Appliance Repair inspect the dryer vent during every gas dryer call?+

A blocked exhaust vent is the leading cause of thermal fuse failure and a documented fire risk. Lint accumulation restricts airflow, causing the dryer to overheat and blow the safety fuse. Replacing the fuse without clearing the vent guarantees a repeat failure. Our technicians measure exhaust back-pressure and clean the vent whenever restriction exceeds the manufacturer limit.

Can a gas dryer produce dangerous carbon monoxide?+

Any gas combustion appliance can produce CO when burning fuel incompletely. A cracked gas dryer heat exchanger, an obstructed exhaust vent, or an improper gas connection can all increase CO output to hazardous levels. Install a carbon monoxide detector near your laundry area. N Appliance Repair tests exhaust CO levels with a combustion analyzer after every gas dryer repair.
