Gas Oven Repair in Toronto — Igniter Failures, Temperature Drift, and Safety Valve Issues

The gas oven igniter is the appliance part we replace more than any other across all Toronto service calls — and it is the repair most frequently misunderstood by homeowners. When a gas oven stops heating, the natural assumption is that something major has failed. In reality, the igniter — a $30 to $60 part — is responsible for over 80 percent of gas oven no-heat complaints. The igniter is a silicon carbide element that must draw a specific amperage (typically 3.2 to 3.6 amps depending on the brand) to energize the bimetal arm inside the gas safety valve. As the igniter ages, its resistance increases and its amperage draw decreases. At some point it drops below the valve's threshold — it still glows visibly orange, giving the homeowner the impression it is working, but the valve never opens and no gas reaches the burner. Our Toronto technicians measure igniter amperage with a clamp meter before recommending replacement, which confirms the diagnosis objectively.

Temperature accuracy is the second most common gas oven concern among Toronto residents. A gas oven that runs 25 to 50 degrees hotter or cooler than the set temperature affects baking results dramatically — collapsed cakes, undercooked roasts, burnt cookies. The causes include a weakening igniter that delays valve opening (shifting the oven's heat cycling pattern), a drifted temperature sensor (the thermistor that reports cavity temperature to the control board), or a gas supply pressure variation that changes the flame characteristics. Toronto homes connected to different sections of Enbridge's distribution network can experience subtle pressure differences, particularly during peak winter demand when gas usage across the city spikes. Our technicians carry a manometer to measure incoming gas pressure at the appliance connection — this simple test reveals supply-side issues that no amount of oven recalibration can fix.

Gas Oven Safety Precautions

A gas oven contains a sealed combustion chamber within your Toronto kitchen. Toronto Fire Services responds to approximately 300 gas-related calls annually across the city. Failed ignition can allow raw gas to accumulate — here is how to respond safely.

  1. If your Toronto gas oven smells like gas but the igniter did not light, turn the oven off and keep the door closed
  2. Ventilate your kitchen — open windows and activate the range hood exhaust
  3. Wait at least 10 minutes before any attempt to use the oven again
  4. If the gas smell continues with all controls off, evacuate and dial 911 from outside
  5. Contact Enbridge Gas at 1-866-763-5427 about the suspected leak

During the extended preheat, small amounts of gas seep past the partially engaged valve and accumulate. Is it dangerous to use a Toronto gas oven with a weak igniter?+

A weakening igniter creates a progressively worsening safety concern. Each preheat cycle allows a small amount of unburned gas to accumulate in the oven cavity before the igniter finally reaches threshold. The result is a noticeable "whump" or flash when the gas ignites — indicating excess gas accumulation. This condition does not improve on its own and should be addressed promptly by a TSSA-licensed technician.
