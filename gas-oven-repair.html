Gas Oven Repair — The Igniter Is Almost Always the Answer

If your gas oven will not heat, there is an 80 percent probability that the igniter glow bar is the culprit. This single statistic — confirmed across thousands of N Appliance Repair service calls — is the most important thing a homeowner can understand about gas oven repair. The igniter is a silicon carbide element that must reach extreme temperature and draw a specific electrical current to trigger the gas safety valve. When it degrades, it glows visibly but cannot generate enough amperage to open the valve. Homeowners see the orange glow and assume the igniter is fine, then pay for an unnecessary gas valve replacement. N Appliance Repair technicians always test igniter amperage first — this one diagnostic step saves our customers hundreds of dollars on misdiagnosed repairs.

Gas Oven Safety Precautions

A gas oven contains a sealed combustion chamber within your Toronto kitchen. Toronto Fire Services responds to approximately 300 gas-related calls annually across the city. Failed ignition can allow raw gas to accumulate — here is how to respond safely.

  1. If your Toronto gas oven smells like gas but the igniter did not light, turn the oven off and keep the door closed
  2. Ventilate your kitchen — open windows and activate the range hood exhaust
  3. Wait at least 10 minutes before any attempt to use the oven again
  4. If the gas smell continues with all controls off, evacuate and dial 911 from outside
  5. Contact Enbridge Gas at 1-866-763-5427 about the suspected leak

How a Gas Oven Igniter Actually Works

The gas oven ignition sequence is elegant in its simplicity but easy to misdiagnose without proper testing:

  1. You set the oven temperature and press Start (or turn the oven knob to the desired temperature)
  2. The control board sends current to the oven igniter through a safety circuit that includes the temperature sensor and high-limit thermostat
  3. The igniter heats up over 60 to 90 seconds, glowing from dark red to bright orange
  4. As the igniter heats, its electrical resistance drops and current flow increases
  5. When current through the igniter reaches approximately 3.2 to 3.6 amps (varies by brand), this current also flows through the bimetal arm in the gas safety valve
  6. The bimetal arm deflects from the heat of this current flow, mechanically opening the gas valve
  7. Gas flows to the oven burner and is ignited by the hot igniter element

The key insight: the igniter does not just light the gas — it also mechanically opens the gas valve through the shared electrical circuit. When the igniter degrades, it draws less current, and the bimetal arm in the valve cannot deflect enough to open. This is why a weak igniter causes a no-heat condition even though the igniter appears to glow normally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does N Appliance Repair always test the igniter first during gas oven repair?+

The igniter is responsible for over 80 percent of gas oven no-heat complaints. It must draw 3.2 to 3.6 amps to mechanically open the safety valve through a shared electrical circuit. A weakened igniter still glows visibly but cannot generate sufficient amperage. Testing this first prevents misdiagnosis and saves homeowners hundreds of dollars on unnecessary gas valve replacements.

What is the difference between the bake and broil igniters?+

Gas ovens have two separate burner assemblies: the bake burner at the bottom of the cavity and the broil burner at the top. Each has its own dedicated igniter. A failed bake igniter means normal oven cooking will not work. A failed broil igniter only affects broiling — baking continues normally. Both igniters cost $150 to $250 to replace.

Can a gas oven produce carbon monoxide?+

Any combustion appliance can generate CO when burning fuel incompletely. A gas oven with a degraded igniter, incorrect air-fuel mixture, or a blocked internal vent can produce CO above safe levels. N Appliance Repair runs a combustion analysis with a calibrated digital analyzer after every gas oven repair, measuring CO concentration and combustion efficiency. We recommend CO detectors near every gas cooking appliance.

What should I do if my gas oven smells like gas during preheating?+

A faint gas smell during the first 60 seconds of preheat can be normal as the igniter warms up. But if the smell persists, or you notice a "whump" sound when the oven finally lights, the igniter is likely degraded. It is drawing enough gas past the safety valve to accumulate before ignition. Turn the oven off and call N Appliance Repair at (437) 524-1053 for same-day diagnosis — this condition worsens progressively.

We repair gas wall ovens, gas double ovens, and combination range ovens from all residential brands. Gas wall ovens require careful handling of the gas supply connection during removal and reinstallation in the cabinet cutout. Double ovens have independent bake and broil igniters for each cavity — up to four igniters total. Our TSSA-licensed technicians are experienced with all configurations.
