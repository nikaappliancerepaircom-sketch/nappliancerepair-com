The igniter is the gas stove component that fails most frequently in Toronto homes, but the specific failure mode depends on the burner type. Stovetop burners use a spark igniter — a small ceramic electrode that produces an electrical arc to light the gas stream. These spark igniters fail when the ceramic cracks (usually from a pot boiling over and thermally shocking the element) or when food debris bridges the electrode gap and prevents a clean spark. The oven burner uses a completely different igniter: a silicon carbide glow bar that must reach approximately 1,800 degrees Fahrenheit to open the safety valve. Oven igniters degrade gradually — they glow dimmer and take longer to open the valve until eventually they cannot reach the required amperage threshold. Both types of igniter failure are same-visit repairs with parts we carry on every Toronto call.

Gas Stove Emergency Response Steps

Gas stoves produce open flames in your Toronto kitchen. Enbridge distributes natural gas to over 500,000 Toronto households. When a gas stove malfunctions, the response protocol differs from electric appliance failures.

  1. Turn all burner knobs on your Toronto gas stove to the OFF position immediately
  2. Do not attempt to relight a burner that has gone out — unburned gas may have pooled above the cooktop
  3. Open kitchen windows and the nearest exterior door for cross-ventilation
  4. If gas odour persists with all controls off, evacuate and dial 911 from outside
  5. Call Enbridge Gas Emergency at 1-866-763-5427 for professional leak investigation
