Samsung ice maker failure is the most common single repair call we receive in Mississauga, by a significant margin. The pattern is predictable: a Samsung French-door refrigerator purchased in 2013 to 2018 stops producing ice after four to six years of operation. The cause in Mississauga is Peel Region's water supply, which carries hardness levels that deposit calcium scale on the ice maker fill valve solenoid at a steady rate. A partially blocked valve produces small or misshapen cubes; a fully blocked valve produces nothing. We carry Samsung fill valve assemblies for every current French-door and bottom-freezer model and complete this repair in a single visit. Descaling the ice maker compartment at the same time extends the time before the next replacement is needed.

Samsung washer error codes — particularly 4E (water supply), 5E (drain), and UE (unbalanced load) — are frequent calls in Mississauga's townhouse developments, where laundry rooms are sometimes in closets with less-than-ideal installation conditions. A 4E error on a Samsung front-load washer in a Mississauga townhouse is often caused by a partially closed supply valve or a kinked supply hose behind the machine — not a valve failure. Before ordering parts, we check every installation variable that could cause these codes, because a thirty-minute investigation often avoids an unnecessary part replacement.

Samsung refrigerator control board failures are increasingly common in Mississauga homes that purchased mid-range Samsung Family Hub or French-door models in the 2015 to 2018 period. These boards control defrost cycles, compressor speed (in variable-speed models), fan operation, and — in Family Hub units — the digital display system. When a control board fails, the symptoms can be varied: a refrigerator that freezes fresh food, a unit that makes ice but stops cooling, or a machine that runs but doesn't defrost properly and eventually develops an ice wall behind the back panel. We test board function systematically and replace boards with genuine Samsung OEM modules.

Samsung dryers in Mississauga homes are generating an increasing volume of heating element calls as the 2013 to 2019 builder-package units age. Samsung heating elements in the DV-series front-load dryers have a documented tendency to fail at the coil support insulators rather than at the coil itself — the insulator cracks under thermal cycling, the coil sags and contacts the element housing, and a short circuit blows the thermal cutoff. We replace the heating element assembly as a complete kit, including the thermal cutoff and cycling thermostat, to ensure the new element doesn't fail on the same fault path.

Samsung dishwashers in the Square One condo corridor frequently display the 7E water wall sensor error and the HC water temperature error. The 7E error is often a false trigger caused by a reflector sensor that has become coated with hard-water scale — cleaning the sensor with a mild acid descaler resolves the error without parts replacement. The HC error indicates that water temperature in the wash cycle is too high, which can be caused by a faulty temperature sensor or, less commonly, a water heater that's set too high. We check both possibilities before recommending sensor replacement.

Local tip for Mississauga Samsung owners: If you have a Samsung front-load washer in a dedicated laundry room in a Mississauga suburban home, check the rubber door boot seal (the bellows gasket that creates the seal between the door and the drum) twice a year. In Mississauga's humid summers, mold grows in the folds of the boot seal more rapidly than in drier climates. A moldy boot seal causes unpleasant odours in laundry and can eventually cause the door glass to crack from seal pressure. Clean the boot seal monthly with diluted bleach; replace it every four to six years.
