Warning Signs — When to Call Immediately in Leaside

  • Gas smell when all burners are off — potential gas leak. Open windows, leave the home, call your gas utility, then call us for stove diagnosis.
  • Yellow or orange flame instead of blue — incomplete combustion producing carbon monoxide. Needs immediate professional attention.
  • Oven igniter takes more than 90 seconds to light — weakening igniter that will fail soon. Preventive replacement costs less than an emergency call.
  • Constant clicking after burners are turned off — spark module short or moisture intrusion. Can be a fire hazard.
  • Glass cooktop crack with visible element — shock hazard. Do not use the affected zone until repaired.

Maintenance Tips for Leaside Stove Owners

  • Clean burner caps and ports monthly: Remove caps and clear ignition ports with a pin. Food debris causes ignition delay and uneven flame.
  • Check gas flex connector annually: Apply soapy water to connections and watch for bubbles. Replace connectors older than 10 years.
  • Use flat-bottomed cookware on glass tops: Warped pots cause hot spots and can crack the glass surface.
  • Limit self-clean cycles: Run the self-clean function no more than once every 3-4 months. The extreme heat stresses control boards and door hinges.
  • Keep the area around the stove clear: Dish towels and paper towels at least 30 cm from the stove — the most common cause of kitchen fires in Canadian homes.
